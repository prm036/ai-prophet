PART 1 — WHAT THE EVIDENCE SAYS (cross-paper synthesis)
================================================================================

1.1 The five things that ACTUALLY move Brier (Pareto-ordered)
-------------------------------------------------------------
Based on the ablation tables in AIA + Kalshibench + Silicon Crowd:

   1. RETRIEVAL — agentic, adaptive, multi-turn search.
      AIA: −0.009 Brier on FB-7-21 (biggest single component).
      Live markets without search vs with: 3.6× Brier difference.
      Caveat: Silicon Crowd shows internet-access barely helps without
      proper retrieval pipeline. Quality > quantity.

   2. PLATT SCALING — fixed α = √3 ≈ 1.73 on aggregated probability.
      AIA: −0.0049 Brier additional after ensembling and supervisor.
      Kalshibench: 90-100% confidence bin is wrong 30-60% of the time
      across all models — Platt is the answer.

   3. ENSEMBLING — multiple independent forecasters, median or mean.
      AIA: M=10 with diminishing returns past 5. Δ Brier ~−0.004 vs single.
      Silicon Crowd: 12 LLMs ≈ human crowd; aggregate is the 2nd-best
      calibrated system measured. But best SINGLE model often beats
      ensemble — ensemble buys CALIBRATION not ACCURACY.

   4. SUPERVISOR / META-REASONER — but with the right pattern.
      AIA: −0.0015 Brier additional. SMALL but structurally important.
      NAIVE meta-reasoning HURTS: AIA shows naive Halawi-style aggregator
      is WORSE than simple mean (0.1168 vs 0.1140). The supervisor must
      (a) read reasoning traces, (b) issue clarifying SEARCH queries,
      (c) output confidence label, (d) fall back to mean if confidence
      isn't "high".

   5. BASE MODEL QUALITY — pick the best one available.
      Kalshibench: Claude Opus 4.5 is the only model with positive BSS.
      All others worse than base-rate baseline.
      Silicon Crowd: GPT-4 alone (0.15) beat the 12-model ensemble (0.20).
      AIA: Sonnet 4 marginally beats o3 on Brier but cutoff overlaps
      benchmark window.
      Net: Anthropic Opus-class is the strongest forecasting LLM today.

1.2 The five things that do NOT help (avoid these)
--------------------------------------------------
   1. EXTENDED REASONING MODE for calibration.
      Kalshibench: 26× more reasoning tokens → 3× WORSE ECE.
      Reasoning chains amplify confirmation bias. Use them for answer
      selection, not for confidence elicitation.

   2. PROMPTING "BE CALIBRATED".
      AIA: largely ineffective for hedging bias.
      Kalshibench: explicit calibration instruction in prompt + still
      systematic overconfidence.
      Math (Platt) is robust; prompts are not.

   3. NAIVE BEST-OF-K LLM PICKING.
      AIA: best-of-k is WORST aggregator tested (0.1191). LLMs are bad
      at picking the best forecast.

   4. NAIVE META-REASONING ("LLM read all forecasts, aggregate").
      AIA: WORSE than simple mean. Model overweights outliers.
      Silicon Crowd: linear pool beats LLM's own Bayesian update.

   5. LET LLM INCORPORATE EXTERNAL SIGNALS DIRECTLY.
      Silicon Crowd Study 2: LLM-after-update is significantly WORSE
      than 0.5*LLM + 0.5*human_median.
      Translation: do market-price blending OUTSIDE the LLM call, not
      inside the prompt. (Our v8 already does this correctly.)

1.3 Lessons specific to Kalshi / Prophet Arena
----------------------------------------------
   • Per-category yes-rates differ wildly (12-67%). Climatology baseline
     should be per-category, not global 50%.
   • Categories where LLMs do WELL: Sports, Entertainment, Elections,
     Financials, Climate.
   • Categories where LLMs do POORLY (~chance): Crypto, Mentions, World,
     Economics.
   • For poor-performing categories, market price is likely much more
     reliable than LLM signal — increase α toward market.
   • OH/WV-style small US primaries are inherently hard (Kalshibench
     would call this "Mentions" or low-data Elections subset).

1.4 The mathematical insight that ties it together
--------------------------------------------------
AIA appendix proves: LOG-ODDS EXTREMIZATION = PLATT SCALING OF GEOMETRIC MEAN.
This means we don't stack two redundant calibration ops — pick ONE:
   Option A: Mean of probs → supervisor → scalar → Platt
   Option B: Geometric mean of probs → extremize (= Platt of geo-mean)
AIA picks A because it allows a supervisor in the middle. We follow A.

2.1 Why this exact ordering
---------------------------
   - Retrieval BEFORE Kalshi: evidence informs the LLM's prior, which is
     then blended with market.
   - Per-agent independent retrieval: paper-supported, captures diversity.
   - Supervisor AFTER agents: gives it access to all reasoning traces;
     allows confidence-based fallback.
   - Linear pool (Step 5) BEFORE guardrail (Step 6): we want guardrail to
     operate on the FINAL combined prior, not pre-market.
   - Platt AFTER guardrail: AIA paper structure (Platt is the last
     statistical correction).
   - Acquiescence correction LAST: tiny constant shift, doesn't fight
     other components.

3.1 Server endpoint
-------------------
   File: server.py (already exists, wired to agent_v8). Upgrade to v9.

   POST /predict:
     Request body: Prophet Arena standard Event JSON
     Response: {"probabilities": [{"market": str, "probability": float}]}
     Internal timeout: 8 min (FORECAST_TIMEOUT_SEC)
     Uniform fallback on any exception or timeout
     Cache by market_ticker with 30-min TTL

   GET /health:
     Returns {"status": "ok", "agent": "v9", "cache_size": N}

3.2 Per-event flow on Prophet Arena
-----------------------------------
1. PA server POST /predict with event JSON.
2. Server invokes agent_v9.predict(event).
3. Inside: STEP 0 ... STEP 9 as above.
4. Returns probabilities.
5. PA scores per-event Brier; aggregates over leaderboard period.

3.3 Caching strategy
--------------------
PA server MAY repeatedly query the same event during a 2-week eval window.
   • First call for market_ticker X: full pipeline, ~30-90s.
   • Subsequent calls within 30 min: serve from cache.
   • After 30 min: re-run pipeline (markets and news may have moved).

For live events where Kalshi price is updating, cache TTL=30 min strikes a
balance between freshness and cost.

3.4 Cost estimate per event
---------------------------
   - Step 1 retrieval: 25 small-LLM calls (~$0.10)
   - Step 3 forecasting: 5 large reasoning calls (~$1.50)
   - Step 4b supervisor: 1 large reasoning call (~$0.50)
   - Step 4b clarifying queries: 1-2 Tavily + 1 LLM (~$0.10)

   TOTAL: ~$2.20 per event
   FOR 200 EVENTS × 1 CALL EACH: $440
   WITH CACHE HITS (~50% revisits): $250-300

If 2-week eval window has more re-queries, budget could go to $500-800.

3.5 Failure modes & graceful degradation
----------------------------------------
   • If Tavily fails: agents proceed with empty brief; guardrail shrinks
     toward uniform.
   • If Kalshi fails: blend skips market prior; LLM-only prediction.
   • If meta-reasoner fails: fall back to median of 5 agents.
   • If individual agent fails: drop it; aggregate over remaining 4 (or
     fewer).
   • If ALL agents fail: uniform fallback (1/N per outcome).
   • If timeout: uniform fallback. Cache invalidated for retry.

PART 6 — RISK ANALYSIS — what could break the win
================================================================================

Risk 1: PROPHET ARENA EVAL DISTRIBUTION ≠ SMOKE TEST DISTRIBUTION.
   Mitigation: Architecture is theoretically motivated, not empirically
   tuned to 26 events. Platt α=√3 from AIA, not learned on smoke.

Risk 2: COMPETITION HAS A TEAM USING AIA-LIKE STACK ALREADY.
   Mitigation: Our advantage is (a) integration of Kalshi historical
   pre-resolution prices (not in any of the papers), (b) per-category α
   tuned to Kalshi distribution, (c) confidence guardrail for thin-evidence
   small-district events.

Risk 3: COST OVERRUNS. $500-800 budget could blow up.
   Mitigation: Cache aggressively (30 min TTL). Set per-event budget cap.
   Adaptive research depth — skip Step 4b supervisor on "easy" events
   (high inter-agent agreement).

Risk 4: SERVER GOES DOWN DURING EVAL WINDOW.
   Mitigation: Fly.io auto-restart. Multi-region if budget allows. Health
   endpoint for liveness check.

Risk 5: PROPHET ARENA SERVER RATE-LIMITS US.
   Mitigation: Concurrency control in server.py (hard_limit=10 in fly.toml).
   FIFO queue. Cache hits return instantly.

Risk 6: NEW PA EVENT TYPES (scalar, conditional) HIT OUR PIPELINE.
   Mitigation: Detect and pass-through with uniform fallback. Log for
   manual review.

Risk 7: KALSHI API CHANGES DURING EVAL.
   Mitigation: Builtin KalshiForecastClient is wrapped behind our kalshi_history.
   Failure mode is graceful (LLM-only fallback).

PART 9 — FINAL ANSWER TO "WHAT ARCHITECTURE WILL WIN?"
================================================================================

The winning architecture for Prophet Arena Forecast track is:

   MULTI-AGENT LLM ENSEMBLE (M=5) with PER-AGENT ADAPTIVE RETRIEVAL,
   reconciled by a META-REASONER with CONFIDENCE-BASED FALLBACK to
   cross-agent MEDIAN, blended with PRE-RESOLUTION KALSHI MARKET PRICES
   via PER-CATEGORY α, gated by a TIERED CONFIDENCE GUARDRAIL on thin
   evidence, finally extremized via PLATT SCALING (α=√3).

   BASE MODELS:
     - 3× Opus 4.7 (different prompts: Calibrated, CoT, ToT)
     - 1× GPT-5 (Calibrated prompt)
     - 1× Gemini 2.5 Pro (Calibrated prompt)
     - 1× Opus 4.7 supervisor (with Tavily clarifying-query access)

   PROMPTS: Halawi-style base-rate-first calibrated prompt with per-category
            yes-rate hint, CoT, and ToT variants. NOT the Tetlock-10-commandments
            version (no evidence it helps).

   RETRIEVAL: Tavily with topic="news", server-side date filter, RFC 2822
              parser, domain blocklist for foreknowledge bias.

   STATISTICAL CORRECTIONS: Platt scaling α=√3 (AIA-recommended fixed value,
                            do NOT tune on small smoke samples).

   SAFETY NETS:
     - Cross-agent median fallback (Silicon Crowd-inspired)
     - Supervisor confidence threshold (AIA-inspired)
     - Tiered guardrail for thin-evidence events
     - Uniform fallback on all errors

   EXPECTED LIVE BRIER: 0.18-0.28 (vs uniform ~0.50-0.65 depending on
                                    Prophet's binary/multi-class mix).

   COMPETITIVE EDGE: Prior work (AIA) is binary-only on academic
   benchmarks; we integrate Kalshi pre-resolution market prices (novel
   for academic papers), per-category α tuned to Kalshi data, and the
   confidence guardrail addresses the niche-event failure mode (OH/WV
   primaries) that AIA / Silicon Crowd / Kalshibench don't address.
