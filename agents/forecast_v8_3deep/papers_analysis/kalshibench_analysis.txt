PART 1 — PAPER SUMMARY
================================================================================

1.1 Key findings (headline)
---------------------------
This is the closest paper to Prophet Arena's data distribution: it evaluates
LLM CALIBRATION on real Kalshi binary markets. The dataset is literally
Kalshi questions.

Top-line result: ALL five evaluated frontier LLMs are SYSTEMATICALLY
OVERCONFIDENT on Kalshi binary forecasts. Accuracy band is narrow (64-69%);
calibration error spans 3× (ECE 0.120 – 0.395). Only Claude Opus 4.5 beats
the climatology base-rate baseline (BSS = +0.057). Every other tested model
has NEGATIVE Brier Skill Score — i.e., worse than just predicting the 40%
base rate.

Most damning finding: REASONING MAKES CALIBRATION WORSE.
GPT-5.2-XHigh uses 26× more reasoning tokens than Claude, achieves similar
accuracy (65.3% vs 69.3%), but has the WORST ECE (0.395) and the WORST
Brier (0.433 — worse than constant 0.40 base-rate prediction).

Headline table:
   Model                   Acc    F1     Brier↓  BSS↑    ECE↓   MCE↓
   Claude Opus 4.5         69.3%  0.676  0.227   +0.057  0.120  0.246  ← only positive BSS
   Kimi-K2                 67.1%  0.633  0.347   −0.446  0.298  0.570
   Qwen3-235B-Thinking     65.7%  0.607  0.346   −0.437  0.297  0.479
   GPT-5.2-XHigh           65.3%  0.599  0.433   −0.799  0.395  0.622
   DeepSeek-V3.2           64.3%  0.614  0.339   −0.407  0.284  0.630

   Reference: superforecasters Brier ~0.15-0.20, ECE ~0.03-0.05
              market crowd Brier ~0.12-0.18

1.2 The benchmark
-----------------
KalshiBench: 300 binary Kalshi prediction-market questions, seed=42 from a
1,531-question full dataset. Temporally filtered to events resolving AFTER
all evaluated models' knowledge cutoffs (latest cutoff = October 2025).
46-day resolution window (very narrow), so news recency is paramount.

13 categories present (out of 16 in full set):
   Sports (83 questions, 27.7%, yes-rate 34.9%)
   Politics (55, 18.3%, 52.7%)
   Entertainment (47, 15.7%, 36.2%)
   Companies (30, 10.0%, 60.0%)
   Elections (24, 8.0%, 20.8%)
   Mentions (19, 6.3%, 36.8%)
   Crypto (11, 3.7%, 27.3%)
   Climate/Weather (9, 3.0%, 33.3%)
   Financials (8, 2.7%, 12.5%)
   World (6), Economics (4), Social (3), Sci/Tech (1)

KEY INSIGHT: yes-rates vary enormously per category (Financials 12.5%,
Companies 60.0%). A uniform 50/50 prior is OBJECTIVELY WORSE than per-category
priors.

Dedup rule: max 2 questions per series_ticker (preserves diversity).
Binary only (no multi-outcome, no scalar markets).
Quality filter: removed ambiguous resolution criteria.

1.3 Models evaluated
--------------------
   Claude Opus 4.5 (Anthropic, cutoff April 2025) — flagship
   GPT-5.2-XHigh (OpenAI, October 2025) — extended reasoning
   DeepSeek-V3.2 (October 2025) — open-weight
   Qwen3-235B-Thinking (June 2025) — reasoning-enhanced
   Kimi-K2 (June 2025) — reasoning-enhanced

NO SCAFFOLDING: single-turn prompt, no retrieval, no tools, no ensembling.
This is a "raw model capability" benchmark, NOT a system benchmark.

System prompt EXPLICITLY says "Be calibrated: if you're 70% confident, you
should be correct about 70% of the time on similar questions." The fact
that models still fail badly means CALIBRATION CANNOT BE PROMPTED INTO THE
MODEL — it has to be applied post-hoc.

Temperature 0.7 standard, 1.0 for GPT-5.2-XHigh (per OpenAI XHigh recommendation).

1.4 The 90-100% bin disaster (most actionable single finding)
-------------------------------------------------------------
When LLMs claim 90%+ confidence in their answer, are they right ≥95% of
the time? NO. In the 90-100% confidence bin:

   Model                Avg Conf  Actual Acc  Gap     N in bin
   Claude Opus 4.5      94.6%     70.0%       +24.6%  20
   DeepSeek-V3.2        93.7%     30.8%       +62.9%  39
   GPT-5.2-XHigh        95.9%     33.7%       +62.2%  104  ← 35% of all preds here!
   Qwen3-235B           94.1%     46.2%       +47.9%  39
   Kimi-K2              94.8%     37.7%       +57.1%  61

Four of five models are WORSE THAN A COIN FLIP when they claim 90%+ confidence.
GPT-5.2-XHigh places 35% of its 300 predictions in this overconfident bin —
and is right only 33.7% of the time there.

Even Claude (the best) is 25 percentage points overconfident in this bin.

PROPHET ARENA IMPLICATION: Any single-model probability ≥ 0.90 must be
treated with extreme skepticism. Post-hoc compression toward the middle is
essential when the raw confidence is in this range.

1.7 What was NOT tested (the open territory)
--------------------------------------------
   ❌ Base-rate priming
   ❌ Retrieval augmentation
   ❌ Self-consistency / multi-sample
   ❌ Multi-model ensembling
   ❌ Chain-of-thought variants
   ❌ Logit-based confidence
   ❌ Reference-class forecasting
   ❌ Post-hoc calibration (Platt, isotonic, temperature)  ← THE BIG ONE

The paper RECOMMENDS post-hoc calibration but does not implement it. This is
the single biggest known opportunity to improve on the paper's numbers.

1.8 Causes of overconfidence (hypothesized)
-------------------------------------------
   1. Training objective: language modeling rewards correct TOKENS, not
      calibrated probabilities.
   2. RLHF pressure: users rate confident responses as more helpful → RLHF
      inadvertently rewards overconfidence.
   3. Hindsight leakage: even temporal filtering leaks via seasonal/recurring
      patterns.

Why reasoning hurts:
   1. Confirmation bias in extended chains — longer = more rationalization.
   2. Verbosity ≠ epistemic humility.


PART 2 — HOW IT WORKS (the evaluation, not the agent)
================================================================================

This paper is an EVALUATION, not an agent system. The "how it works" is:

For each of 300 questions:
   1. Pull question text + description + category from Kalshi.
   2. Pass to one of 5 frontier LLMs with the calibration-instructing prompt.
   3. Parse the output: <answer>yes/no</answer> AND <confidence>0-100</confidence>.
   4. Compute Brier, BSS, ECE, MCE, OCR@τ vs ground truth.

The "result" is the calibration measurement.

Practical takeaway for AGENT design: at every probability the LLM emits,
note that the raw verbalized confidence is unreliable. The 90-100% bin is
the most dangerous. The model can be PROMPTED to be calibrated and still
fail — only post-hoc statistical correction works.


PART 3 — TRANSLATION TO PROPHET ARENA FORECAST TRACK
================================================================================

This paper is the MOST DIRECTLY APPLICABLE of the three because Prophet
Arena IS Kalshi (mostly). The lessons transfer almost 1:1.

3.1 Concept-level translations
------------------------------
THE BENCHMARK IS LITERALLY OUR DATA DISTRIBUTION. 300 Kalshi binary
questions from late 2025. Prophet Arena has Kalshi binary + multi-outcome
events resolving May 17-31 2026. Same source, same categories, same
calibration challenges.

KEY CONCEPT: "Calibration cannot be prompted in." We've been adding
"be calibrated" / "avoid overconfidence" / Halawi-style guidance to v8's
prompt. The paper shows this MARGINALLY HELPS but doesn't fix the
underlying RLHF bias. Mathematical post-hoc correction (Platt) is the
real fix — which we've now done.

KEY CONCEPT: "Reasoning hurts calibration." This is alarming for our v8
which uses reasoning mode (`reasoning.effort=medium`) on Opus, o3,
DeepSeek-R1. The paper's evidence: 26× more reasoning tokens → 3× WORSE
ECE.
   → We should test v8 WITHOUT reasoning mode and compare.
   → Or use reasoning mode only for the answer-selection step, and elicit
     confidence from a non-reasoning pass.

KEY CONCEPT: "Per-category yes-rates differ wildly." We have per-category
α (Sports 0.70, Politics 0.50, Entertainment 0.35, Elections 0.30) but
these are for Kalshi BLEND, not for the LLM prior itself. We could ALSO
add per-category base-rate ANCHORS to the LLM prompt:
   "Note: in this category, the historical yes-rate is X%."

3.2 Technique-level changes to v8
---------------------------------
Change 1: Confirm Claude Opus 4.5 IS our default in v8.
   Currently we use anthropic/claude-opus-4.7 (a successor to 4.5). Per
   this paper, Opus is the SOLE model that beats base rate. Reinforces our
   choice. Keep Opus as the dominant ensemble member.

Change 2: De-prioritize the reasoning mode for confidence elicitation.
   The paper shows reasoning models OVER-CONFIDENCE more. Our v8 currently
   uses reasoning=medium on Opus/o3/DeepSeek-R1/Gemini. Consider:
      a) Setting reasoning.effort=low for confidence pass
      b) Using reasoning for ANSWER but separate non-reasoning for confidence
      c) Apply MORE AGGRESSIVE Platt scaling to compensate

Change 3: Add per-category base-rate to prompt context.
   In agent_v8._format_user_prompt(), insert:
      "Historical yes-rate in this category: <X>%"
   Build the per-category yes-rate table from the Kalshibench full dataset
   (huggingface.co/datasets/2084Collective/kalshibench-v2 — 1,531 examples).
   Use it as a Bayesian prior anchor.

Change 4: Tune Platt scaling against Kalshibench, not just our 26-event
sample. The Kalshibench HF dataset gives us 1,531 labeled examples — n=58×
larger than our smoke set. This is enough to FIT a real per-category Platt
curve (not just one global α).
   Fit per-category α on Kalshibench → apply to v8 by category.

3.3 Prophet API handling
------------------------
   • Categories in Prophet Arena events come from the `category` field. The
     Kalshibench categories MATCH our v8 categories. Direct mapping.
   • For multi-outcome Prophet events (e.g. NHL Calder w/ 30 outcomes),
     Kalshibench doesn't have a direct test — it's binary only. We must
     generalize multi-class Platt + per-category α ourselves (we have).
   • The Brier on Kalshibench's Claude Opus 4.5 is 0.227. That's roughly
     half of our v8's 0.408 on the 26-event smoke. The gap is because our
     26 includes 3-5 catastrophic-fail events (OH-15, WV-01, ToC) that
     dominate the average. Take heart: the underlying model is strong;
     our pipeline failures are concentrated.
   • Yes-rate calibration: Prophet Arena events likely have a similar
     yes-rate spread to Kalshibench (~40% overall, 12-67% per category).
     A uniform climatology baseline is bad; per-category is better.

3.4 Specific recipes worth copying
----------------------------------
   • Confusion matrix monitoring: all 5 KalshiBench models UNDER-PREDICT
     YES (high false-negative rate). Our v8 may have the same issue. If
     observed on live eval data, add an inverse-acquiescence shift.
   • OCR@τ (overconfidence rate at threshold τ): track this on our predictions.
     If we predict ≥0.90 on Outcome X, the historical empirical rate of
     Outcome X being correct should be ≥90%. If not, we're overconfident.
   • The HF dataset (2084Collective/kalshibench-v2): pull it as a held-out
     calibration set. Run our agent_v8 on it, fit Platt, validate. This is
     a far more principled tuning ground than our 26-event sample.


PART 4 — PROS AND CONS for adopting Kalshibench insights
================================================================================

PROS
----
1. CLOSEST DATA MATCH to Prophet Arena of the 3 papers (same source: Kalshi).
2. VALIDATES OUR MODEL CHOICE (Claude Opus dominates other frontier LLMs
   on calibration on Kalshi data).
3. PROVIDES A REAL CALIBRATION SET (1,531 examples on HF) that's 58× larger
   than our 26-event smoke.
4. DOCUMENTS PER-CATEGORY YES-RATES, which we can use as priors directly.
5. CONFIRMS PLATT IS THE ANSWER for the overconfidence bin (90-100%) where
   single models fail catastrophically.
6. WARNS US about reasoning-mode pitfall before live eval.

CONS
----
1. PAPER IS AN EVALUATION, NOT A SYSTEM. No agent architecture to lift.
2. BINARY ONLY — doesn't cover multi-outcome Prophet events (Calder, ToC,
   Roast subject) which were the worst in our smoke test.
3. NO RETRIEVAL TESTED — paper deliberately tests RAW model capability. The
   95% performance gap to humans is partly because retrieval is missing.
4. 300 QUESTIONS is small. Variance across models could be sample artifact.
5. CATEGORY BREAKDOWN ONLY DONE FOR CLAUDE — can't tell if other models
   have category-specific failure modes.
6. RESOLUTION WINDOW NARROW (46 days). Doesn't test long-horizon forecasts.
7. NO MARKET-PRICE BASELINE in the paper — we still don't know the Kalshi
   midpoint's Brier on these specific 300 questions. (We strongly suspect
   market price would be the toughest baseline to beat.)
