PART 4 — IS KALSHI MARKET PRICE FULLY DEBIASED?
================================================================================

The question: when we fetch Kalshi market price for the smoke test, do
we get a PRE-RESOLUTION (debiased) price, or do we accidentally get a
POST-RESOLUTION (answer-key) price?

OUR SETUP (kalshi_history.py):
  - cutoff_dt = real_resolution_date - 3 days
    (real_resolution_date is MANUALLY RESEARCHED per event, NOT Kalshi's
     close_time. e.g. Hungary election real_resolution=2026-04-12, even
     though Kalshi close_time was 2026-05-09.)
  - We call /trade-api/v2/series/.../markets/{ticker}/candlesticks
    asking for hourly OR daily candles up to cutoff_dt
  - We return the price at the CANDLE COVERING cutoff_dt (or just before)

DEBIASED? YES for retrospective smoke test, with caveats:
  ✓ The candle price at cutoff_dt is what the market thought AT THAT
    TIMESTAMP. It cannot incorporate events that happened after.
  ✓ For Hungary: cutoff = April 9, election = April 12. Magyar's victory
    not yet realized; market price reflects pre-election odds (likely
    leaning Magyar based on polls, but not 0.99-confident).
  ✓ For OH primary: cutoff = May 2, election = May 5. Pre-election.
  ✓ For multi-day events (cricket May 8-12): we set
    real_resolution=match-start (May 8), so cutoff=May 5 = pre-match.

  POTENTIAL BIAS SOURCES we cannot fully eliminate:
  ◐ INFORMED BUT PRE-RESOLUTION market price: even pre-resolution, if
    the market knew (e.g., overwhelming poll lead, injury news), the
    price reflects that. This is LEGITIMATE forecaster knowledge, not
    answer-key leakage. NOT a bias to remove.
  ◐ Candlestick aggregation level: hourly candles aggregate over an
    hour; if huge news broke mid-hour, the closing price of that hour
    reflects post-news prices. Still pre-cutoff overall.
  ◐ Liquidity: thin-market candles can be wildly noisy. We treat them
    as data anyway (no liquidity filter).

LIVE MODE (cutoff_dt = None or in future):
  We use CURRENT (yes_bid + yes_ask) / 2 from the live market. Cannot
  leak because the event hasn't resolved yet. Bias-free by construction.

VERDICT: ✓ Kalshi prices are FULLY DEBIASED for our smoke test, in the
sense that they reflect only information available before our chosen
cutoff. The candle endpoint serves historical OHLC data; we pick the
right historical timestamp; no post-resolution information bleeds in.

The ONE case where bias COULD sneak in: if real_resolution_dates.json
has a wrong date. E.g., if we said "Hungary real_resolution = 2026-05-09"
(matching Kalshi close), the candle at May 6 would include 4 weeks of
post-election trading — that WOULD be leaked. We mitigated this with
manual research per event. Re-confirm by spot-checking 2-3 entries in
real_resolution_dates.json against the actual event date.
