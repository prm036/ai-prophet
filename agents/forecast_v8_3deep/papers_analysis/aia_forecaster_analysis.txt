PART 1 — PAPER SUMMARY
================================================================================

1.1 Key findings (headline)
---------------------------
First verified LLM-based forecasting system to match expert human superforecasters
at scale on real-world events. On the ForecastBench benchmark:

  • FB-7-21 (498 questions):
      AIA = 0.1076   Superforecasters = 0.1110   Prior LLM SOTA = 0.133
      OpenAI o3 (no scaffolding) = 0.1221
  • FB-8-14 (602 questions):
      AIA = 0.1099   Superforecasters = 0.1152
  • FB-Market (76 questions):
      AIA = 0.0753   Superforecasters = 0.0740   Market consensus = 0.0965
  • MarketLiquid (1610 questions, their novel harder benchmark):
      AIA = 0.1258   Market = 0.1106 (AIA loses to market alone)
      But AIA+Market ensemble = 0.106 (beats both — diversifying signal)

Statistically indistinguishable from superforecasters (paired p≈0.10).

1.2 Architecture (3 pillars)
----------------------------
Pillar 1 — Agentic individual forecasters (M = 10):
  Each independent agent does iterative adaptive search:
      q → E₁ → E₂ → ... → Eₙ → (reasoning R_i, probability p_i)
  Multi-turn, adaptive, agent chooses what to query and when. Each forecaster
  outputs both a probability AND its reasoning trace.

Pillar 2 — Supervisor reconciliation:
  Reads all M reasoning traces. Identifies disagreements between forecasters.
  Issues NEW search queries to resolve those disagreements (base-rate lookups,
  fact-checks). Outputs aggregated forecast + a confidence label
  (high / medium / low). Only "high-confidence" supervisor updates override
  the simple mean; medium/low fall back to the simple mean of the M forecasts.

Pillar 3 — Statistical correction (Platt scaling):
  Applied to the supervisor's final scalar output:
      p_final = sigmoid(α · log(p/(1-p))) = p^α / (p^α + (1-p)^α)
  Fixed α = √3 ≈ 1.73 (do NOT tune on benchmark; learned optimum ≈ 2.27
  gives only marginal extra gain but risks overfitting).

1.3 The math (key derivations from Appendix G.2)
-------------------------------------------------
Multi-class Platt: p_i → p_i^α / sum_j(p_j^α)
General form with shift: p → sigmoid(α·log(p/(1-p)) + log d)

CRITICAL THEOREM proved in this paper for the first time: log-odds
extremization IS Platt scaling applied to the geometric mean of forecasts.
That is, the Tetlock-style extremization formula

      log(p̂/(1-p̂)) = (a/n) · Σᵢ log(pᵢ/(1-pᵢ))

is mathematically equivalent to Platt scaling of the geometric mean of {pᵢ}
with parameter a, shift = 1. This means "extremization" and "Platt on geo-mean"
are NOT two separate techniques to stack — they're the same technique.

For an AIA-style system that uses a supervisor, Platt is the better choice
than extremization because it operates on the supervisor's scalar output (so
you can keep the supervisor in the pipeline). Extremization would require
removing the supervisor.

1.4 Models used
---------------
Default: OpenAI o3 (October 2023 knowledge cutoff — well before benchmark
events, eliminates pretraining lookahead bias). Used in three roles:
forecaster, supervisor, and foreknowledge-bias judge.

Model comparison on MarketLiquid (no supervisor, Brier):
   Claude Sonnet 4 = 0.1195 (best, but knowledge cutoff overlaps benchmark)
   OpenAI o3       = 0.1242  ← chosen as default for clean cutoff
   Qwen-32b        = 0.1404
   GPT-5           = 0.1412
   Gemini 2.5 Pro  = 0.1456
   GPT-4o          = 0.1485

Claude Opus 4 excluded as "prohibitively slow."
OpenAI OSS-120b appeared best but turned out to be exploiting foreknowledge
bias (17% of queries leaked outcome info).

1.5 Datasets / benchmarks
-------------------------
• FB-7-21:    498 binary questions, resolution range 7/28/2024 – 12/31/2050.
              Original Karger ForecastBench.
• FB-8-14:    602 binary questions, snapshot of 08/14/2025.
• FB-Market:  76 questions sourced from prediction markets (subset of FB-7-21).
• MarketLiquid: 1610 questions, novel benchmark built from a popular public
              prediction market. Politics/policy/econ/AI only. Markets must
              be ≥1 week open with ≥5,000 contracts traded. 322 underlying
              markets × 5 sampling dates per market.
• MarketNightly: ~250/night on live OPEN markets — immune to foreknowledge
              bias (resolution unknown at query time).

1.6 Search strategy details
---------------------------
"Agentic, adaptive" search beats fixed-template retrieval substantially.
On FB-7-21 (no supervisor, no Platt):
   No search:                      Brier 0.1230
   Non-agentic search (3 queries): Brier 0.1217
   Agentic search:                 Brier 0.1140

Two search providers tested ("Search-A" natural-language; "Search-B" web-page
lists). Search-A wins consistently because it returns dense answers, not link
lists.

Foreknowledge bias mitigation: dedicated LLM-as-judge step that reads search
snippets + reasoning citations and flags possible leakage. High-recall, low
precision. Audit on 502 traces: ~1.65% residual bias rate. Blocklist
maintained for high-leakage domains (FIDE Wikipedia, WeatherSpark, Macrotrends,
NASDAQ market-activity, etc.).

1.7 Component ablation (cumulative Brier reduction on FB-7-21)
--------------------------------------------------------------
   No-search baseline:          0.1230
   + Agentic search:            0.1140  (−0.0090)  ← biggest single component
   + Supervisor:                0.1125  (−0.0015)
   + Platt scaling (α=√3):      0.1076  (−0.0049)  ← second biggest

Ensemble vs single (10 vs 1):   0.1140 vs 0.1182  (−0.0042)

The ENSEMBLE is the dominant Brier-mover. Search and Platt are next. The
supervisor adds smaller value but is structurally important because it (a)
catches outliers that would otherwise drag the mean and (b) enables Platt to
be applied to a scalar.

1.8 What does NOT work (key negative findings)
----------------------------------------------
• Naive "best-of-k" (LLM picks one of k forecasts): 0.1191 — barely better
  than a single forecast. LLMs are bad at picking the best.
• Naive meta-reasoning ("LLM reads all forecasts and aggregates"): 0.1168 —
  WORSE than simple mean (0.1140). The LLM overemphasizes outliers.
• Prompting to "be calibrated": LARGELY INEFFECTIVE for hedging bias.
  Mathematical corrections (Platt) are far more robust.

1.9 Live-market experiments (immune to foreknowledge bias)
----------------------------------------------------------
On 64 closed real markets:
   AIA with search:    Brier 0.1002
   AIA without search: Brier 0.3609  (3.6× worse than with search)
   Market consensus:   Brier 0.1111

So agentic search makes a 3.6× live-market Brier difference. AIA slightly
beats market consensus, though n=64 is small.

1.10 Limitations
----------------
• Binary, unconditional events only. Multi-class ("which sector wins?"),
  point estimates ("what will CPI be?"), and conditional forecasts are
  explicitly deferred.
• MarketLiquid (harder benchmark): AIA at 0.126 still loses to market at
  0.111.
• Foreknowledge bias is endemic to search APIs (live widgets, continuously
  updated pages); can't be fully eliminated.
• Authors caution against high-stakes deployment without human oversight.


PART 2 — HOW IT WORKS (Operational explanation)
================================================================================

For one question:

Step 1 — Spawn 10 parallel agents. Each runs independently. No coordination.

Step 2 — Each agent gets a search tool. The agent decides on its own when
         to issue a query, what to query, and when it has enough information.
         A typical agent runs 3-8 queries iteratively, with each subsequent
         query informed by what the prior search returned. It then produces
         (probability, reasoning).

Step 3 — Supervisor agent reads all 10 reasoning traces and 10 probabilities.
         It DOES NOT just average. Instead:
            (a) Identifies where forecasters disagree
            (b) Generates targeted "clarifying" queries (e.g., base rate
                lookups, fact-checks)
            (c) Runs those queries
            (d) Emits its own final probability + a confidence label

Step 4 — Decision rule on the supervisor's output:
            - If supervisor confidence = "high" → use supervisor's probability
            - If "medium" or "low"             → use simple mean of the 10
         This protects against the "LLM overweights outliers" failure mode.

Step 5 — Apply Platt scaling with fixed α = √3 to the scalar from Step 4.
         This sharpens probabilities along the sigmoid (e.g., 0.6 → 0.66,
         0.85 → 0.93). Fixes the LLM RLHF-induced hedge-to-0.5 bias.

Step 6 — Output final probability.


PART 3 — TRANSLATION TO PROPHET ARENA FORECAST TRACK
================================================================================

Prophet Arena = real-time event slate (Kalshi-sourced markets, mostly binary
or multi-outcome categorical). Our agent receives a POST /predict per event.
Response must be {"probabilities": [{"market": <outcome>, "probability": float}]}.

3.1 Direct translations (1:1)
-----------------------------
ALL three pillars apply directly:

PILLAR 1 (M parallel agents):
  → Our v8 already has 5 parallel agent slots (Opus/CoT, Opus/ToT,
    Opus/Calibrated, GPT-5/Calibrated, Gemini/Calibrated).
  → AIA paper recommends M=10 with diminishing returns past 5. Our 5 is
    defensible. If cost permits, double Opus slots to 8 (5 with different
    prompts × different temperature settings).
  → Each agent should have its OWN search tool, not share one summary.
    Currently we share one Tavily summary across all 5 — this LOSES
    diversity. Per-agent independent retrieval would help.

PILLAR 2 (Supervisor):
  → Our v8 meta-reasoner does this — but is currently weaker than AIA's:
       - We pass ALL 5 agent probs + reasoning to one Opus call
       - But we DO NOT give the meta-reasoner a search tool to run
         clarifying queries
       - We DO NOT have a confidence label that decides "use supervisor
         vs fallback to mean"
  → Upgrade: give the meta-reasoner Tavily access for clarifying queries,
    add the confidence-label fallback.

PILLAR 3 (Platt scaling):
  → Done. v8 has FORECAST_PLATT_A = 2.0 default. AIA recommends α = √3
    ≈ 1.73 (slightly lower). On our 26-event smoke test our sweep showed
    monotonic improvement to α=8, suggesting our sample is too small to
    pick the right α reliably. The AIA-recommended α=√3 may transfer
    better to live eval.
  → Decision: lower our FORECAST_PLATT_A from 2.0 → 1.73 to match the
    paper's evidence-based value. We can always retune on accumulating
    live data during the eval window.

3.2 Concept-level recipes to lift
---------------------------------
  • Use OpenAI o3 as one ensemble member. Its October 2023 cutoff means
    less risk of pretraining lookahead bias. (We already added o3 in v7
    Phase 1; keep in v8.)
  • Foreknowledge bias is less of a concern for Prophet Arena LIVE eval
    because events resolve in the FUTURE — there's no answer to leak.
    BUT for the smoke-test on settled events, we already addressed this
    with per-event real_resolution_dates + Tavily date filter.

3.3 Technique-level changes to v8
---------------------------------
  Change 1: Lower Platt α to √3 ≈ 1.73
     File: agent_v8.py line ~80
     Edit: FORECAST_PLATT_A default 2.0 → 1.73

  Change 2: Give meta-reasoner a search tool
     File: agent_v8.py _meta_reason() function
     Before invoking the meta-LLM, give it the option to call Tavily for
     1-2 clarifying queries. Add a tool-use loop. Cost: +1-2 LLM calls per
     event.

  Change 3: Add supervisor confidence label + fallback
     The meta-reasoner outputs not only probabilities but also
     `confidence: "high"|"medium"|"low"`. If "medium"/"low", we use the
     simple mean of the 5 agent probabilities instead.

  Change 4: Per-agent independent retrieval (not just shared summary)
     Currently all 5 agent slots see the same Tavily brief. AIA paper
     emphasizes that diversity in research paths is part of why ensembling
     works. Give each agent its own Tavily query budget (2-3 queries each).
     Cost: 5× Tavily calls per event, but each is free under 1000/mo.

3.4 Prophet API handling (concrete)
-----------------------------------
  • POST /predict on our server.py:
      Takes the standard Event JSON from Prophet Arena
      Returns the standard {"probabilities": [...]} shape
      No changes needed to the API surface — all AIA-style improvements
      live INSIDE agent_v8.predict().

  • Server timeout: AIA's pipeline (M=10 with agentic search) is slower
    than ours. With supervisor + Platt our budget is ~5-8 min per question.
    Prophet Arena's expected timeout window is 10 min per request.
    Mitigation: agent slots run in parallel; supervisor sequential after.
    Cache by market_ticker so repeated requests during eval return fast.

  • Backoff: live markets can be queried multiple times. If supervisor
    confidence was "low" last call, but we now have new evidence, the
    cache key should incorporate a time bucket (we already do 30 min TTL).

3.5 Specific recipes worth copying
----------------------------------
  • Foreknowledge-judge prompt (Appendix D of AIA paper) — adapt for our
    smoke-test bias audits. We already do post-hoc date filtering; could
    add LLM-as-judge as a backstop.
  • Domain blocklist: FIDE rankings Wikipedia, WeatherSpark, Macrotrends,
    NASDAQ market-activity. Apply at Tavily query time as `exclude_domains`.
  • Bootstrap-resampling at question level for pairwise p-values during
    development comparisons.


PART 4 — PROS AND CONS for adopting AIA pattern in Prophet Arena
================================================================================

PROS
----
1. PROVEN: matches superforecasters on ForecastBench, the closest public
   benchmark to Prophet Arena. This is the only LLM forecasting system
   verified to do so.
2. EVERY COMPONENT is independently validated by ablation. Search is biggest
   (~−0.009), ensembling next (~−0.004), Platt (~−0.005), supervisor smallest
   (~−0.0015). Each can be added incrementally.
3. PLATT SCALING IS FREE. Pure math, no LLM calls. Already integrated.
4. SUPERVISOR FALLBACK pattern (high → use; low → fall back to mean) is
   robust and easy to implement.
5. MATHEMATICAL CONNECTION (extremization = Platt of geo-mean) means we
   don't accidentally stack two redundant operations.
6. EVIDENCE-BASED HYPERPARAMETERS: α=√3, M=10. Not tuned on our small smoke
   set, less likely to overfit.

CONS
----
1. COST: M=10 forecasters + 1 supervisor + retrieval per question is
   expensive. Our v8 already at $1-2/event with 5 slots. M=10 would 2×.
   For ~200 events over the eval window = $400-800.
2. LATENCY: Sequential supervisor-after-parallel pattern adds 30-60s
   end-to-end. Prophet Arena 10-min timeout is generous, but per-pod
   throughput suffers.
3. PROPHET ARENA HAS MULTI-OUTCOME EVENTS (e.g. NHL Calder, ToC). AIA paper
   only validated binary. Platt extension to multi-class is what we did
   (p_i^α / sum_j p_j^α) — mathematically reasonable but not validated by
   the paper.
4. RELIANCE ON o3 OR SIMILAR REASONING MODEL: AIA used o3 as default. Our
   v8 has Opus 4.7 as the strongest reasoning model. Empirically Opus does
   better on Brier in our tests but uses more tokens — cost concern.
5. THE 0.30 GAP WE'RE FIGHTING: AIA reports Brier 0.10-0.13 on benchmarks
   that are similar to Prophet Arena. We're at 0.39 on smoke test. Most of
   that gap is from THIN-EVIDENCE / WRONG-CONFIDENT events (OH/WV primaries,
   ToC, etc.) which AIA doesn't address either. No magic bullet here.
6. THE SUPERVISOR-WITH-TOOLS UPGRADE adds engineering complexity, risk of
   tool-call loops, and increased latency. Worth doing but not trivial.
