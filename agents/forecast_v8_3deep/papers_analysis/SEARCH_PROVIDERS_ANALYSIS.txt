PART 1 — WHAT AIA LABS USED (per arXiv 2511.07678)
================================================================================

The AIA paper deliberately ANONYMIZES its two search providers:
   • ProviderA — "focuses primarily on serving natural language user queries"
                 → returns synthesized snippets / direct answers
                 (Perplexity / Tavily / Brave style)
   • ProviderB — "intended to integrate directly with agentic AI systems"
                 → returns web-page lists for the agent to navigate
                 (Exa / Linkup style)

AIA ALSO had an "internal corpus of high-quality news data" as a
supplementary source. We do not have access to this — Bridgewater
proprietary asset.

Headline findings from AIA paper Section 4.1 (Table on search methodology):

   No search:                  Brier 0.1230
   ProviderB non-agentic:      Brier 0.12168
   ProviderB agentic:          Brier 0.11824
   ProviderA non-agentic:      Brier 0.11738
   ProviderA agentic:          Brier 0.1140   ← WINNER

⇒ ProviderA (NL-synthesis style) clearly beats ProviderB (link-list style),
  consistently, across agentic and non-agentic search loops.
⇒ Agentic search consistently beats non-agentic, given the same provider.
⇒ Search is the single biggest performance lever the paper identifies:
  3.6× live-market Brier difference between with-search and no-search
  (0.1002 vs 0.3609).


PART 2 — WHAT WE USE (current Prophet Arena agent_aia.py)
================================================================================

We use ONLY Tavily, configured as follows:
   • Provider: tavily-python SDK
   • Endpoint: tavily.search(query, max_results=5, topic="news",
                              start_date=..., end_date=...,
                              exclude_domains=...)
   • Filters applied:
       - topic="news"  (news articles only)
       - start_date / end_date  (smoke-test mode: real_resolution-90d to
                                 real_resolution-3d)
       - exclude_domains: AIA paper blocklist (Wikipedia, weatherspark,
         macrotrends, nasdaq, historique-meteo, tipranks, tradingeconomics)
   • Per-query result limit: 5 articles
   • Per-agent search budget: up to 4 iterations (max_iters_forecaster)
   • Supervisor search budget: 3 iterations

Cost: TAVILY_API_KEY is on the free tier (1000 calls/month). With M=10
agents × 4 iters × 26 events = 1040 queries to validate the smoke test
alone — we'd hit the limit. In practice many agents stop early; we're
not actually hitting 1000 yet.

Tavily IS a ProviderA-class system — natural-language oriented, designed
for LLM consumption, returns synthesized snippets with title / URL /
content / published_date. This is what AIA paper says is the right
class.

PART 6 — WHAT THE PAPERS RECOMMEND ABOUT MULTIPLE PROVIDERS
================================================================================

AIA paper §4.1: "Agentic search significantly outperforms non-agentic
search, and conditional on the search methodology, ProviderA consistently
outperforms ProviderB."

⇒ Single ProviderA-class provider, agentic loop, is the validated recipe.

The paper does NOT test multi-provider ensembling. Their internal news
corpus is layered ON TOP of ProviderA, but not via the agent — it's a
separate retrieval surface. We don't have an equivalent.

Silicon Crowd paper §6: shows internet access is not statistically
significant in their ensemble (with 12 LLMs). But their setup did NOT
use proper agentic search.

Halawi 2024 (referenced by both papers): a single retrieval API with
news-summarization is the standard. No multi-provider experiments.

⇒ No literature strongly motivates running multiple search providers in
parallel. Adding a second provider is engineering complexity without
documented Brier benefit.


PART 7 — RECOMMENDATION FOR PROPHET ARENA
================================================================================

GIVEN:
   • We have ~10 hours to deadline
   • OpenRouter budget $18 remaining (Tavily is on free tier, separate)
   • Current agent_aia uses Tavily, working, validated on 8 events
   • Search is the single biggest performance lever per AIA paper

RECOMMENDATION: STICK WITH TAVILY (our current setup). Here's why:

1. TAVILY IS THE RIGHT PROVIDER-CLASS.
   • ProviderA-equivalent per AIA paper's anonymization → the validated
     winner.
   • Natural-language synthesis, news-focused, dates-bearing, exclude-
     domains support, start_date/end_date support — every feature we
     need.

2. ALREADY INTEGRATED. Switching costs ≥ 30 min of work + revalidation
   on 26 events ($5-15) we can't afford to lose. Switching to a
   marginally better provider gives ~0.005 Brier improvement at best
   (AIA paper Table 3); switching to Anthropic web_search means
   refactoring our LLM auth path.

3. FREE TIER COVERS EVAL. 1000/mo Tavily free tier is roughly:
     200 events × M=10 × ~2 queries = 4000 calls/mo expected.
   We are over the free tier ceiling at full M=10 load. Either pay
   ~$5-20 for an upgrade OR cut to M=5. Mitigation: cache by query.

4. ANTHROPIC web_search WOULD BE A NICE-TO-HAVE BACKUP. If Tavily fails
   or runs out, having Claude's own web_search as a secondary tool the
   agent can call would give us redundancy. BUT this requires switching
   our LLM auth path from OpenRouter to direct Anthropic API for the
   relevant calls — ~1-2 hours of work, risky before deadline.

5. ASKNEWS NOT WORTH IT WITHOUT PARTNERSHIP. $99/mo for ~0.02 Brier
   improvement (estimated). Better spent on OpenRouter top-ups.

6. WE COULD ADD EXA AS A FALLBACK. If Tavily returns 0 results for a
   query, the agent could automatically retry with Exa. ~30 min of work.
   Useful but not critical. DEFER unless we have time after deploy.

PART 9 — FINAL ANSWER
================================================================================

Q1: What does AIA Labs use?
   A: Anonymized "ProviderA" (natural-language synthesis class, e.g.
      Perplexity/Tavily) + their proprietary internal news corpus.

Q2: What do we use?
   A: Tavily (ProviderA-class equivalent), free tier, with date filter +
      AIA blocklist. M=10 agents do agentic search loops; each agent
      gets up to 4 search iterations.

Q3: Should we switch?
   A: NO. Tavily is the right class. Switching to Perplexity (~$20 over
      eval) or Anthropic web_search (~$0 but 1-2 hr refactor) gives
      ~0.005 Brier improvement at best per AIA paper's evidence — not
      worth the risk before deadline. Adding Exa as fallback is a
      post-deploy stretch goal.

Q4: AskNews?
   A: NO. Without the Metaculus AIB partnership, AskNews is $99/mo for
      marginal improvement. Tavily is the more cost-effective option for
      a hackathon team without commercial partnership.
